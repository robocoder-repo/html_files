Best Practices for Digital Privacy and Security

Implementing these best practices can significantly improve your digital privacy and security. Remember, cybersecurity is an ongoing process, not a one-time task.

Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Create complex passwords using a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a different password for each account to minimize the impact of a potential breach.

Tips for creating strong passwords:

  • Use at least 12 characters
  • Include numbers, symbols, capital and lowercase letters
  • Avoid using personal information or common words
  • Consider using a passphrase instead of a single word
  • Use a password manager to generate and store complex passwords

Remember, never share your passwords with anyone, and avoid using the same password across multiple accounts.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Whenever possible, enable 2FA on your accounts. This adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second form of verification in addition to your password.

Common types of 2FA:

  • SMS-based: A code is sent to your phone via text message
  • App-based: Use an authenticator app like Google Authenticator or Authy
  • Hardware tokens: Physical devices that generate codes
  • Biometric: Fingerprint or facial recognition

While any form of 2FA is better than none, app-based or hardware token methods are generally more secure than SMS-based 2FA.

Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system, applications, and antivirus software to ensure you have the latest security patches and features.

Best practices for software updates:

  • Enable automatic updates whenever possible
  • Regularly check for updates manually if automatic updates are not available
  • Update all devices, including smartphones and tablets
  • Be cautious of fake update notifications - always download updates from official sources

Outdated software can have vulnerabilities that cybercriminals can exploit. Keeping your software up-to-date is one of the easiest ways to protect yourself.

Be Cautious with Public Wi-Fi

Avoid accessing sensitive information when connected to public Wi-Fi networks. If necessary, use a VPN to encrypt your connection.

Tips for using public Wi-Fi safely:

  • Use a VPN to encrypt your internet traffic
  • Avoid accessing sensitive accounts (e.g., banking, email) on public Wi-Fi
  • Disable automatic connections to public Wi-Fi networks
  • Use your mobile data instead of public Wi-Fi when possible
  • Ensure HTTPS is enabled when browsing websites

Remember, even if a public Wi-Fi network requires a password, it's still not secure unless you're using additional protection like a VPN.

Use Encryption

Encryption is a process that scrambles your data so that only authorized parties can understand it. Use encryption whenever possible to protect your data.

Ways to use encryption:

  • Use HTTPS websites for browsing (look for the padlock icon in your browser)
  • Enable full-disk encryption on your devices
  • Use encrypted messaging apps for sensitive communications
  • Encrypt your email using PGP or S/MIME
  • Use a VPN to encrypt your internet traffic

Encryption is particularly important when transmitting sensitive information over the internet or storing it on devices that could be lost or stolen.
